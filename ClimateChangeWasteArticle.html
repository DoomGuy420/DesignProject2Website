Climate Change and Waste

Burning fossil fuels happens everyday and in turn, carbon dioxide or CO2 is emitted into the atmosphere every second. This is really bad for the Earth as the Carbon Dioxide acts as a screen, allowing in sunlight, but trapping some of it when the sunlight tries to reflect back off of the Earth. This in turn creates a greenhouse effect and warms up the Earth and causes much more problems for the Earth. It is extremely important to change our habits to prevent this.
2 / 3
The effects of climate change are very dire. Rising temperatures, hurricanes and weather intensifying, rising sea levels and the melting of the Arctic are all part of the effects of climate change. There are huge consequences if we do not work towards stopping this problem. Thankfully, people are working towardsstopping it. It is a group effort the whole world must take part in. It is really important that we all do what we can to stop climate change.

As seen in the graph, the changes in the temperature has been hovering at roughly 1 celcius above regular temperatures. While at first glance this seems like a tiny problem, it has huge consequences. 0.5 degrees celcius makes a huge difference, in wildlife, plantlife, access to water and ecosystems. Insects would lose roughly half their population along with plants and vertebrates. Along this, if the world were to jump from 1.5 celcius, it would go from 350 million people with water shortages and being exposed to drought, to 410 million. As seen, these effects are hugely impactful, and it is imperative, that we stop climate change before it ruins the planet.
